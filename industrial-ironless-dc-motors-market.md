<p><p>Industrial ironless DC motors come in two primary types: brushless motors and brushed motors. Brushless motors utilize electronic commutation, resulting in higher efficiency, reduced maintenance, and longer lifespan, making them suitable for demanding applications. In contrast, brushed motors employ mechanical brushes for commutation, which can lead to wear and require more maintenance but are generally simpler and more cost-effective for less demanding tasks. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs, impacting performance, reliability, and operational costs.</p></p>
