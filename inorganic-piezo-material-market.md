<p><p>The inorganic piezo material market primarily features two types: piezoelectric crystals and piezoelectric ceramics. Piezoelectric crystals, such as quartz, exhibit direct piezoelectric properties and are known for their high sensitivity and stability. In contrast, piezoelectric ceramics, primarily composed of lead zirconate titanate (PZT), offer greater versatility and are widely used in various applications due to their enhanced piezoelectric response and manufacturability. Both materials are essential in sensors, actuators, and transducers across industries like healthcare, automotive, and aerospace.</p></p>
