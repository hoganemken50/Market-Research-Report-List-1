<p><p>The carbon and graphite market is divided into two primary types: natural and synthetic graphite. Natural graphite is sourced from mining operations, characterized by its occurrence in three forms: flake, lump, and amorphous. It is commonly used in batteries, lubricants, and refractories. Synthetic graphite, produced from petroleum products through high-temperature processes, offers superior purity and performance, making it ideal for high-tech applications like electric vehicles and semiconductors. Both types play crucial roles across various industries, each serving specific needs.</p></p>
