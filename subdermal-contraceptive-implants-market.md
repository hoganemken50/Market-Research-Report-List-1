<p><p>Subdermal contraceptive implants are a form of long-acting reversible contraception that involves the insertion of flexible plastic rods under the skin, typically in the upper arm. These rods release hormones, primarily progestin, to prevent ovulation and thus reduce the likelihood of pregnancy for several years. The increasing awareness of family planning, rising prevalence of unintended pregnancies, and a growing emphasis on women's health are driving the demand for these contraceptive methods.</p><p>The market for subdermal contraceptive implants is experiencing robust growth, projected to expand at a CAGR of 10.9% during the forecast period. Enhanced distribution channels, including clinics and pharmacies, are also facilitating greater accessibility, further supporting the uptake of subdermal contraceptive implants.
